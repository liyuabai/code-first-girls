London's Green Spaces

It can feel difficult to find some time to yourself in the bustling streets of London. 33% of London is actually public green space, so you are never too far away from finding somewhere to clear your mind and talke a moment for yourself. On this page we want to sahre with you some of our favourite green spaces.

South London

Battersea Park

Battersea Park is a 200 acre park that has many secluded areas. In the spring, the pathways are lined with cherry blossoms and you can promenade along the riverfront.

Mindful tip

Check out the Buddhist London Peace Pagoda, which features four large gilded bronze sculptures of Buddhas overlooking the Thames. The perfect place to practice meditation as the sun sets over the Thames.

Brockwell Park

Brockwell Park is a much-needed slab of green just south of Brixton. Locals from Herne Hill, Tulse Hill and Brixton flock here in summer to sun-worship, fly kites, swim in the outside pool.

Mindful tip

Make a splash at Brockwell Lido and admire its Grade II-listed art deco buildings, which have been at the centre of park life since the 1930s.

Dulwich Park

Situated right in the centre of Dulwich, this park is charming and peaceful. In summer the park is filled with wild flowers to delight the senses.

Mindful tip

Hire a boat and punt around the lake; let the soothing water calm your soul.

North London

Clissold Park

This charming park in Stoke Newington has long been a local favourite. There’s a small farm, Grade II listed Clissold House (though it’s off bounds to most), skatepark, five-aside and table tennis areas dotted throughout 55 acres of lush outdoor space.

Mindful tip

Take a walk from this lovely part into Abney Park Cemetary which offers many secluded walks through dappled woodland.

Primrose Hill

Perched at the top of the much bigger Regent’s Park, Primrose Hill is a pretty stretch of parkland with views out south to central London. It’s one of London’s six protected viewpoints – so one to head for if you want to admire the city’s sprawling skyline. The stone atop the summit of the hill says it all “I have conversed with the spiritual sun. I saw him on Primrose Hill,” a fitting quotation from William Blake.

Mindful tip

Highgate Cemetery is one of the Magnificent Seven Cemeteries that were built outside of central London in the 19th century. It’s the best known of those seven – with a number of famous occupants ranging from George Eliot to Karl Marx and Christina Rosetti.

Hampstead Heath

Hampstead Heath needs no introduction. The 791 acres of meandering woodlands, open spaces and bathing ponds come together to create one of the best-known London parks. Of course, you should grab an eyeful of the views from Parliament Hill and see the gorgeous Hampstead Pergola but once you’re done with that, take the time to explore this extensive stretch of countryside that’s been surrounded by urban sprawl.

Mindful tip

Take a swim in Hampstead Heath’s Ice cold ponds - swimming in cold water has been thought to lower anxiety and increase a sense of calm.

West London

Holland Park

A place for flower lovers, Holland Park was the first place in the UK to grow Dahlias, there are still many beautiful and fragrant gardens to explore. In the summer months the park plays host to the Holland Park Opera Festival.

Mindful tip

Take a walk from this lovely part into Abney Park Cemetary which offers many secluded walks through dappled woodland.

Chiswick House

Surrounding a grand manor that was inspired by the Earl of Burlington’s time spent in Italy, the gardens of Chiswick House are easily one of the best West London parks. Perfectly landscaped grass, stone archways, and Roman statues transport you straight to the Amalfi Coast. Swoon.

Mindful tip

Head to the cafe for some real Italian espresso. You can use your senses of smell and taste to ground yourself in this lovely garden

Richmond Park

No doubt, you’ll have spotted the friendly deer that live in this park all over Instagram, and it’s these pretty creatures that make the park so popular. As well as the wildlife, there are nature trails, the gorgeous Isabella Plantation, and incredible views of St Pauls from King Henry’s Mound.

Mindful tip

This park is undoubtedly big enough to spend a day exploring all its corners. Why not take a day off from work and life to spend getting some space in Richmond Park.
